## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Hybrid — Content Generation Pipeline + Structured Extraction (multimodal)

**Description:**
CriaLook campanha-ia is a marketing-asset generation pipeline for Brazilian fashion lojistas. A single user request produces (a) one photorealistic VTO image of the lojista's chosen virtual model wearing the lojista's product, (b) a structured Sonnet-generated PT-BR (or EN) marketing copy with hashtags, CTAs, captions for Instagram Feed/WhatsApp/Stories, and (c) a structured Gemini visual analysis of the product. "Good" means: identity-preserved photorealistic VTO with no anatomical errors and faithful garment/color/wash reproduction; copy that respects the brand glossary, uses an explicit mental trigger, and never invents product attributes (size, fabric, color) it cannot see in the photo.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
1. **Identity drift in VTO** — generated face is a "similar" person, not the lojista's chosen model; or hair color/length/skin tone shifts silently.
2. **Garment fidelity loss** — denim wash drift (dark indigo → medium blue), missing brand mark on chest/sleeve, color shift, fabric texture flattening, missing pieces in a `conjunto`.
3. **Copy invents attributes** — Sonnet writes "disponível do P ao GG" when sizes were never provided; claims "afina a cintura em 2 cm" (transformative claim, CONAR risk); names a fabric ("seda") it cannot visually verify.
4. **Anatomical errors in VTO** — extra/missing fingers, fused fingers, melted hands; cropped body when full-body was required; bare feet on non-beachwear.
5. **Silent prompt regression** — a prompt edit on Tuesday degrades copy quality measurably by Friday and there is no signal, no version pin, no rollback path.

## 1b. Domain Context

**Industry Vertical:** Brazilian small-business fashion retail (lojista, social-commerce-driven) — Instagram Feed/Stories + WhatsApp Business as the dominant channel mix; R$ pricing; PT-BR copy default.

**User Population:** Lojistas of small fashion stores (typically ~5-50 SKUs/month, often a solo founder or 2-3-person team). They are *not* professional copywriters and *not* agency users — they want a "post-ready" caption + image they can publish in one tap. Many will glance and post; some will post without reading.

**Stakes Level:** **Medium-High.** Not life-or-death (vs healthcare/legal), but real and direct: a campaign with the wrong garment color or an invented size damages the lojista's customer trust and drives returns; a CONAR-violating claim (e.g. "afina a cintura em 2 cm", "tira celulite") creates real legal exposure for the lojista as the *anunciante* and reputational exposure for CriaLook as the tool that produced the copy.

**Output Consequence:** AI output is posted directly to Instagram Feed/Stories and WhatsApp Business by the lojista. **There is no human review step in the current product flow** — a generated caption + image goes from the SSE stream to a "Copy" / "Post" action with no compliance gate, no second-eye review, no scheduled-post queue. This is the single most important consequence to internalize for evaluation: **any guardrail that fails ships to the public**, and the public belongs to the lojista's audience (real consumers making purchase decisions in BRL). The downstream economic impact of a single bad generation is small (one post deleted, one customer lost) but the cumulative effect across thousands of lojistas is brand-defining for CriaLook.

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

> These EXTEND the 5 critical failure modes in Section 1 with domain-specific manifestations the audit surfaced. They are not duplicates of Section 1 — they are the Brazilian-fashion-specific shape that those generic categories take.

- **Denim-wash drift on the highest-volume SKU category.** Section 1 lists "denim wash drift" as an instance of garment-fidelity loss. The domain-specific severity is that **affordable jeans is the #1 category for Brazilian lojistas**; a wash error on jeans hits more campaigns than any other garment type and erodes trust on the SKU customers are most likely to compare against competitors' photos.
- **PT/EN bilingual prompt drift (silent semantic divergence).** Section 1 lists "silent prompt regression". The domain-specific shape is: PT and EN system prompts are maintained as parallel strings (`sonnet-copywriter.ts` locale switch). A glossary update — say, adding "delavê" to the denim-wash list — lands in PT but not in EN, and EN generations silently degrade with no test gate. D-13 explicitly defers consolidation; the failure mode is therefore live until Phase 2.
- **Anti-cliché violations at scale.** Section 1's "copy invents attributes" sits next to a quieter failure: the model defaults to ChatGPT-generic Portuguese ("Confira esta peça incrível!") under low-context inputs. For a lojista's audience this reads as obviously AI-generated and underperforms organic engagement benchmarks for Brazilian fashion accounts (Instagram fashion engagement medians per Zeeng 2025 are already low; falling below them is product-defining).
- **Identity-drift on lojista's *chosen* model.** Section 1 lists generic "identity drift". The domain-specific severity in CriaLook is that lojistas explicitly pick a model image they identify with their brand persona; the generated VTO drifting to a "similar" face breaks that brand-persona contract specifically (vs a generic "wrong face" failure in a stock-photo product). This is the main intersection between the technical failure mode and the LGPD posture in D-12.

CriaLook ships a **tool**; the lojista is the **anunciante** under Brazilian advertising self-regulation (CONTEXT.md decision D-12). ## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** Bare SDKs — `@anthropic-ai/sdk` + `@google/genai` (no orchestration framework).

**Versions (currently pinned):**
- `@anthropic-ai/sdk@^0.92.0`
- `@google/genai@^1.48.0`
- `zod@^4.3.6` (boundary validation, already in deps)

**Rationale:**
The pipeline today is three deterministic LLM calls (Gemini analyzer → parallel Gemini VTO + Sonnet copy) with no agentic loop, no dynamic tool use, no provider-swap requirement, and no retrieval. Every framework worth considering — Vercel AI SDK, Mastra, LangChain, LangGraph, Inngest Agent Kit — adds an abstraction layer (provider router, agent loop, workflow runtime) whose value is amortized over many LLM calls or many providers. Three calls and two providers don't pay for that overhead. The pipeline already has best-in-class custom infrastructure for what it actually needs (`callGeminiSafe` retry+classify wrapper, structured `responseJsonSchema`, real-token cost accounting), and migrating that to a framework would be a regression in error handling fidelity. Critically, Vercel AI SDK does not yet treat Gemini 3 Pro Image as a first-class image-generation provider — the VTO call would remain bare-SDK regardless, defeating the unification argument.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| Vercel AI SDK | Gemini 3 Pro Image not first-class for image gen; would still need bare SDK for VTO; partial unification has negative ROI |
| Mastra | Full framework adoption (workflows + evals + tracing native) is a swing too large to justify against 3-call pipeline; eval and observability solved more cheaply by direct DB-write to existing `campaign_scores` + Phoenix later |
| LangChain / LangGraph | Heavy abstraction tax; debugging through chains is harder than through bare SDK calls; no agentic flow requires the graph |
| Inngest Agent Kit | We already use Inngest for async jobs — Agent Kit specifically is for agent loops we don't have |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Partial — Anthropic for copy, Google for vision and image. Lock-in is by-design (each model is best-in-class for its task) and an ADR (`ADR-AI-FRAMEWORK.md`) documents revisit triggers (≥4 LLM calls; any agentic loop; new provider; >2 prompt-quality regressions in 90 days).

### Entry Point Pattern — Sonnet `tool_use` (highest-leverage new pattern)

This replaces the regex `JSON.parse` block at `sonnet-copywriter.ts:181-194`. The model is forced to emit a `tool_use` content block whose `input` matches the schema we declare; we still validate at the boundary with Zod because schema enforcement at the LLM layer is best-effort, not contractual.

```ts
import Anthropic from "@anthropic-ai/sdk";
import { z } from "zod";

const client = new Anthropic({ maxRetries: 2 });           // SDK retries 408/409/429/5xx by default

const SonnetDicasPostagemSchema = z.object({
  caption_sugerida: z.string().min(1),
  legendas: z.array(z.object({
    foto: z.number().int().positive(),
    plataforma: z.string(),
    legenda: z.string().min(1),
  })).min(3),
  // ...rest of fields mirroring SonnetDicasPostagem interface
});

const response = await client.messages.create({
  model: "claude-sonnet-4-6",
  max_tokens: 1500,
  temperature: 0.7,
  system: SYSTEM_PROMPT,
  tools: [{
    name: "generate_dicas_postagem",
    description: "Returns the structured marketing-copy package for one fashion product photo.",
    input_schema: {
      type: "object",
      properties: {
        caption_sugerida: { type: "string" },
        legendas: { type: "array", items: { type: "object", properties: {
          foto: { type: "integer" },
          plataforma: { type: "string" },
          legenda: { type: "string" },
        }, required: ["foto", "plataforma", "legenda"] }, minItems: 3 },
        // ...
      },
      required: ["caption_sugerida", "legendas" /* ...rest */],
    },
  }],
  tool_choice: { type: "tool", name: "generate_dicas_postagem" },  // force this tool
  messages: [{ role: "user", content: contentParts }],
});

const block = response.content.find((b) => b.type === "tool_use" && b.name === "generate_dicas_postagem");
if (!block || block.type !== "tool_use") throw new SonnetInvalidOutputError("no tool_use block");
const dicas = SonnetDicasPostagemSchema.parse(block.input);     // throws ZodError on shape drift
```

### Pitfalls (specific to this codebase)

1. **Don't `await` the SDK client constructor.** `new Anthropic({...})` is synchronous and cheap; the lazy singleton at `sonnet-copywriter.ts:57-64` is the right pattern. After D-10 the singleton lives in `lib/ai/clients.ts` only — do not re-instantiate inside hot paths.
2. **The TypeScript SDK's default request `timeout` is 10 minutes.** That is far above our `maxDuration = 300` Vercel route cap. Do not rely on the SDK timeout for liveness — it will silently let a Gemini hang eat the entire request budget. Wrap with `withTimeout(promise, ms, label)` (D-17). Keep the SDK timeout default for non-streaming completion safety, but do not depend on it.
3. **`tool_choice: { type: "tool", name: "..." }` prefills the assistant turn.** The model emits `tool_use` blocks only — no leading natural-language text, no chain-of-thought. This is exactly what we want for a structured-output replacement, but it means any "explain your reasoning" you had in the system prompt stops working. Move reasoning into the `description` field on the tool itself, or accept that for this phase reasoning was never logged anyway.
4. **`tool_use.input` is `unknown` at the type level.** TypeScript will not save you. Always pipe through `Schema.parse(block.input)` — never `block.input as MyType`. The Zod throw is the boundary; `SonnetInvalidOutputError` re-wraps it with Sentry context (`store_id`, `campaign_id` are already on the upstream error breadcrumb in `route.ts`).
5. **Singletons must use lazy module-level `let`, not top-level `const`.** Top-level `const new Anthropic({...})` evaluates at import time, before `process.env` is hydrated in some Next.js 16 edge cases (Inngest cold start, vitest with `setupFiles`). Keep the `let _x: T \| null = null; if (!_x) ...` pattern from `sonnet-copywriter.ts:56-64`.

const SonnetDicaLegendaSchema = z.object({
  foto: z.number().int().positive(),

### 4.2 Generic timeout wrapper (D-17)

```ts
// campanha-ia/src/lib/ai/with-timeout.ts
export class AITimeoutError extends Error {
  readonly code = "AI_TIMEOUT" as const;
  readonly retryable = true;
  readonly userMessage = "A IA demorou demais para responder. Tente novamente.";
  constructor(public readonly label: string, public readonly timeoutMs: number) {
    super(`[${label}] timeout after ${timeoutMs}ms`);
  }
}

export function withTimeout<T>(promise: Promise<T>, timeoutMs: number, label: string): Promise<T> {
  let timer: ReturnType<typeof setTimeout> | undefined;
  const timeout = new Promise<never>((_, reject) => {
    timer = setTimeout(() => reject(new AITimeoutError(label, timeoutMs)), timeoutMs);
  });
  return Promise.race([promise, timeout]).finally(() => {
    if (timer) clearTimeout(timer);   // important: prevents "open handle" warnings + Vercel idle leak
  });
}
```

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

### 4b.1 Structured Outputs with Zod (TypeScript equivalent of Pydantic)

This codebase is TypeScript; Zod is the boundary validator (`zod@^4.3.6`, already in deps — no new dependency). The pattern for the two providers:

**Anthropic:** `tools[]` + `tool_choice: { type: "tool", name }` constrains the LLM to emit a `tool_use` block whose `input` follows the JSON Schema. Then `Schema.safeParse(block.input)` is the contract. See Section 4.1 for the full code.

**Google GenAI:** `generationConfig.responseMimeType: "application/json"` + `responseJsonSchema: <schema>` is already in use in `gemini-analyzer.ts:316-329`. Wrap the parsed JSON in `Schema.safeParse(JSON.parse(text))` at the boundary — the schema-mode generation is best-effort, not contractual.

**Single source of truth.** The Zod schema is the type — derive the TypeScript interface with `z.infer` rather than maintaining two declarations. The pre-existing `SonnetDicasPostagem` interface should be redefined as `export type SonnetDicasPostagem = z.infer<typeof SonnetDicasPostagemSchema>` once the schema lands.

**Retry policy.** Anthropic's SDK already retries transport errors twice (`maxRetries: 2` default). For *schema* failures (Zod throws), do NOT retry blindly — a schema failure is a content problem, not a transport problem; retry burns money without changing the outcome. Throw `SonnetInvalidOutputError`, surface to Sentry via `captureError`, fall through to the existing `dicas` fallback at `pipeline.ts:218-237`. The `regenerate_reason` enum from D-01 will tell us in production whether `copy_wrong` correlates with these errors.

**What to log on a Zod failure:**
- `SonnetInvalidOutputError.technicalMessage` (Zod issue paths joined)
- `block.input` (the raw model output)
- `response.stop_reason` (was it `end_turn`, `tool_use`, `max_tokens`, or `refusal`?)
- `prompt_version` from D-15 (which prompt revision produced this?)
- `store_id` and `campaign_id` (already on the upstream Sentry breadcrumb)

### 4b.2 Async-First Design — Next.js 16 App Router + SSE

Both SDKs are Promise-based; everything in this pipeline is `async/await`. The Next.js 16 App Router runs route handlers on the Node.js runtime (not Edge — `/api/campaign/generate/route.ts` writes to Supabase + uses Sharp). The pipeline orchestrator at `pipeline.ts` runs three calls — analyzer first, then VTO and Sonnet **in parallel via `Promise.all`** — already correct; do not regress to sequential.

**The one Next.js-specific mistake to avoid:** never pass an async generator or a `for await` loop directly through to a `Response` body. The current code uses an explicit SSE writer (`route.ts` SSE pattern) and that stays. The SDK's `messages.stream()` helper is *not used* in this pipeline because Sonnet output is structured (a `tool_use` block) — you can't stream-validate a partial JSON object against Zod, so we `await` the full response. **Stream the progress (analyzer done, VTO done, copy done) over SSE; await the LLM calls themselves.** That split is intentional.

**Race-condition trap with `withTimeout`.** If the timeout wins the race but the underlying fetch is still in flight, you leak a connection. The `.finally(() => clearTimeout(timer))` in 4.2 only clears the timer; it does not abort the fetch. For the Anthropic SDK, pass `{ signal: ac.signal }` on the request options + `ac.abort()` in a future hardening pass — out of scope this phase, but flagged here so the next person doesn't think the leak is mysterious. For Vercel serverless this is bounded by `maxDuration` so it's not a process-level leak in production, but it can keep a vitest run hanging locally.

**`Promise.all` failure semantics.** If analyzer succeeds and VTO fails, Sonnet's promise is still pending — `Promise.all` rejects on the first failure. Use `Promise.allSettled` if you want both rejections logged independently; the current code uses `Promise.all` and that's acceptable because the route-level catch logs whichever rejected first to Sentry with full breadcrumbs.

### 4b.3 Prompt Engineering Discipline

- **System vs. user separation is already correct** — `system: buildSystemPrompt(locale)` carries the persona/rules; `user.content` carries the image + per-request inputs (price, store name, target audience). Do not collapse.
- **Few-shot lives inline in the system prompt** today (the "✅ exemplo correto / ❌ exemplo errado" blocks). This is the right call for now: one prompt, deterministic, cacheable. When DOMAIN-RUBRIC.md (D-11) lands, the few-shots come *from* the rubric — but they stay inline in the prompt string, the rubric is the human-review document, not a runtime dependency (D-14).
- **Always set `max_tokens` explicitly.** Sonnet today sets `1500`, Gemini analyzer caps at `8192`. Do not leave unbounded — Anthropic's SDK calculates a default timeout from `max_tokens` and a high value pushes timeout past the route cap. Code-review rule: any new `messages.create` without `max_tokens` is a blocker.
- **`prompt_version` (D-15) is the discipline gate.** A 12-char SHA of the system prompt computed at module load (`crypto.createHash('sha256').update(SYSTEM_PROMPT).digest('hex').slice(0, 12)`) gets written to `api_cost_logs.metadata.prompt_version`. This is what makes "did the Tuesday prompt edit cause Friday's quality dip?" answerable — without it, every regression debug starts from zero.

### 4b.4 Context Window Management

Not applicable in the conventional sense — this isn't RAG, isn't multi-turn conversational, isn't autonomous. Sonnet sees one image + ~3-5KB of system prompt; Gemini analyzer sees the same. Both well below their windows (200k Claude / 1M Gemini). 

**One thing to *not* do:** do not start passing analyzer JSON output back into Sonnet to "give it more context". Sonnet already does its own visual analysis (this is by design — read the comment block at `sonnet-copywriter.ts:1-14`). Cross-feeding would (a) double the cost, (b) introduce a coupling that bilingual prompt sync (D-13 deferred) would have to track, (c) re-introduce the failure-mode where Sonnet hallucinates from analyzer mistakes instead of from its own (more accurate) visual reading.

If the pipeline ever grows to ≥4 LLM calls (one of the ADR revisit triggers), context-window patterns become relevant — until then, this section is "leave it alone".

Existing observability surface is `api_cost_logs` (one row per LLM call, with `metadata jsonb`). After D-18 there is exactly one helper, `logModelCost`, that writes there. After D-15 every row carries `metadata.prompt_version`. No new tracing infra this phase — Phoenix/Langfuse is Phase 3 (Observability).

**Caching.** Not used today, not added this phase. ## 6. Guardrails

> Online guardrails run on EVERY request and add latency to a route already operating inside a `maxDuration = 300` budget. Keep the online list short, cheap, code-based. Push everything else to the offline flywheel. Per CONTEXT.md D-04: Phase 1 captures signal; *acting* on signal (alerts, rollbacks) is Phase 2.

### 6.1 Online (Real-Time) Guardrails

| # | Guardrail | Trigger | Intervention | Cost | Phase |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Schema validation (Zod)** on Sonnet `tool_use.input` | Every Sonnet response | Throw `SonnetInvalidOutputError`; `pipeline.ts:218-237` falls through to default `dicas` shape; campaign still completes. | <1ms | **`[Phase 1]`** — already in scope per D-16 |
| 2 | **Input sanitization** (`safeStr` in `route.ts`) | Every campaign-generate POST | Strip control chars, length-cap fields, reject empties before LLM call. | <1ms | **`[Phase 1]`** — already shipping in `route.ts` |
| 3 | **Forbidden-token regex** on Sonnet output (`captionsugerida`, `caption_alternativa`, `legendas[].legenda`) | Every Sonnet response (post-Zod, pre-DB-write) | If any forbidden token matches: log `regenerate_reason_auto="forbidden_token"` to `api_cost_logs.metadata`, fall through to `pipeline.ts:218-237` default `dicas`. List: invented sizes (`/\b(do\s+P\s+ao\s+GG|tamanhos?\s+P\s+ao\s+GG)\b/i`), transformative body claims (`/\b(afina\s+a?\s*cintura|tira\s+celulite|modela\s+o\s+corpo|rejuvenesce)\b/i`), invented fabric weight (`/\b(seda\s+gelada|seda\s+pura|cetim\s+puro)\b/i` — only triggers if lojista didn't input `tecido`). | <2ms (compiled regex, ~5-10 patterns, ~3KB caption text) | **`[Phase 1]`** — cheap, catches the highest-stakes Section 1b failures (Compliance-safe + Garment-attribute) before they ship. The online forbidden-token regex catches the *catastrophic* CONAR/invented-attribute failures; nuanced quality grading is offline.

### 7.4 Smart Sampling Strategy

| Phase | Strategy |
|---|---|
| **`[Phase 1]`** | **Surface ALL `regenerate_reason` rows in `/admin/custos`** for ad-hoc human review by product owner. Volume is bounded by total regenerates (today ~5-10% of campaigns) so 100% review is tractable. The PT-BR copywriter (Section 1b) is *not yet engaged* in Phase 1 — her labeling cadence starts when the golden set begins curation in Phase 2. |
| **`[Phase 2]`** | **Sample 10/week of "concerning" generations** for the PT-BR copywriter to grade against the rubric. Selection rule (priority order): (a) campaigns with LLM-judge `nota_geral ≤ 2`, (b) campaigns with `regenerate_reason="copy_wrong"` and a `prompt_version` deployed in the last 7 days, (c) campaigns where LLM-judge and `regenerate_reason` *disagree* (judge says good, lojista regenerated — high-value calibration data). Her grades become new golden-set entries. |
| **`[Phase 3]`** | **Anomaly-driven sampling from Phoenix spans** — automatically flag traces 2σ above rolling latency or token-count means; flag spans where `prompt_version` distribution shifted in the last 24h. Adds an "investigate this trace" queue to the Phase 2 weekly review. |
